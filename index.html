The Journey

Computing Through the Decades

Explore the key milestones that transformed computing from room-sized machines to devices that fit in our pockets.

1940s

First Electronic Computers

ENIAC, the first programmable, electronic, general-purpose digital computer, was completed in 1945. It weighed 30 tons and filled an entire room.

1950s

Commercial Computers

The UNIVAC I became the first commercially available computer in the United States. IBM dominated the business computing field with its IBM 700/7000 series.

1960s

Minicomputers & Integrated Circuits

The introduction of integrated circuits led to the minicomputer revolution. DEC's PDP-8 was a groundbreaking minicomputer that cost a fraction of mainframes.

1970s

Personal Computing Begins

The Altair 8800, introduced in 1975, sparked the personal computer revolution. Apple, founded in 1976, released the Apple II in 1977, one of the first successful mass-produced microcomputers.

1980s

PC Revolution

IBM introduced its Personal Computer (PC) in 1981, setting a standard for the industry. Microsoft's MS-DOS became the dominant operating system for PCs.

1990s

Internet Era

The World Wide Web was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 and released to the public in 1991. The browser wars began, and the dot-com boom took off.

2000s

Mobile Revolution

Portable computing devices became smaller and more powerful. Apple's iPhone, launched in 2007, revolutionized mobile computing and set the stage for the app economy.

2010s

Cloud & AI Era

Cloud computing became mainstream. Machine learning and artificial intelligence made significant advances, with applications in voice recognition, image processing, and natural language understanding.

2020s

Quantum & Beyond

Quantum computing advances, with practical quantum computers beginning to solve problems impossible for classical computers. AI systems reach new milestones of capability.

Key Breakthroughs

Innovations That Changed Everything

These pivotal technologies fundamentally transformed computing and our relationship with technology.

1947

Transistors

Replaced vacuum tubes, making computers smaller, faster, and more reliable. The invention of the transistor at Bell Labs in 1947 revolutionized electronics.

1958

Integrated Circuits

Combining multiple transistors onto a single chip dramatically reduced size and cost while increasing processing power and reliability.

1973

Graphical User Interface

The GUI made computers accessible to non-specialists, using visual metaphors like windows, icons, and menus instead of command-line interfaces.

1989

World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee's invention connected the world's computers through hypertext, creating the foundation of the modern internet experience.

2007

Mobile Computing

Smartphones combined computing, communication, and sensors into pocket-sized devices that revolutionized how we interact with technology.
